# AI for Safer Roads Innovation Challenge 2026

## Project Title

Safe System Speed Assessment Using Explainable AI

## Challenge

Asian Development Bank (ADB) AI for Safer Roads Innovation Challenge 2026

---

## Objective

This project evaluates whether existing speed limits are appropriate for the surrounding road environment and identifies locations where speed-management interventions may improve road safety.

The solution follows Safe System principles and combines explainable machine learning with geospatial analysis to support evidence-based decision making.

---

## Methodology

### Safe System Framework

The assessment is based on Safe System concepts, recognizing that road users make mistakes and that road environments should be designed to minimize the likelihood and severity of crashes.

### Data Processing

Road network, speed, traffic exposure, and contextual attributes were processed and validated prior to analysis.

### Speed Safety Assessment

Each road segment was assigned:

- Speed Safety Score
- Risk Category
- Priority Score

### Explainable AI

Machine learning models were used alongside SHAP explainability techniques to identify the factors most strongly associated with elevated speed-related risk.

### Risk Prioritization

Priority scores combine:

- Safety risk
- Exposure
- Potential intervention impact

allowing authorities to focus on locations with the greatest potential safety benefit.

---

## Key Outputs

The solution generates:

- Speed Safety Scores
- Risk Categories
- Priority Rankings
- Interactive GIS Visualizations
- Explainable AI Insights
- Policy Recommendations

---

## Innovation

The framework moves beyond predicting historical speed limits.

Instead, it evaluates whether current speeds are appropriate for the surrounding road environment and supports proactive intervention planning.

Key innovations include:

- Safe System integration
- Explainable AI
- Risk-based prioritization
- GIS decision support
- Scalable methodology

---

## Transparency and Interpretability

Model outputs are supported by:

- Feature importance analysis
- SHAP explanations
- Risk prioritization logic
- Documented assumptions

This improves transparency and supports practical implementation.

---

## Limitations

- Crash history data was not available.
- Exposure measures are proxy indicators.
- Recommendations support engineering judgement rather than replace it.
- Local context should be considered before implementation.
